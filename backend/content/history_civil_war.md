# The American Civil War

## A Nation Divided

The Civil War (1861-1865) was fought between Northern and Southern states over slavery and states' rights.

**Key Facts:**
- Lasted from 1861 to 1865 (4 years)
- Fought between the Union (North) and Confederacy (South)
- Main cause was disagreement over slavery
- Deadliest war in American history (over 600,000 deaths)
- Resulted in freedom for enslaved people

**The Two Sides:**

**Union (North):**
- 23 states
- Led by President Abraham Lincoln
- Wanted to keep the United States together
- Against the expansion of slavery
- Industrial economy (factories, railroads)
- Larger population

**Confederacy (South):**
- 11 states that seceded (left the Union)
- Led by President Jefferson Davis
- Wanted states' rights and independence
- Economy based on slavery and agriculture (cotton, tobacco)
- Fought to preserve slavery

**Why Did It Start?**

**Main Issue: Slavery**
- Southern economy depended on enslaved labor
- Northern states had abolished slavery
- Debate: Should new states allow slavery?
- Southern states feared losing power

**Trigger: Lincoln's Election (1860)**
- Abraham Lincoln opposed slavery's expansion
- Southern states didn't wait to see what he'd do
- 11 states seceded and formed the Confederacy
- War began when Confederates fired on Fort Sumter (April 12, 1861)

**Major Battles:**

**1. Battle of Gettysburg (July 1863)**
- Turning point of the war
- Fought in Pennsylvania
- Union victory
- Bloodiest battle (51,000 casualties)
- Lincoln gave famous Gettysburg Address here

**2. Battle of Antietam (September 1862)**
- Bloodiest single day in American history
- Led to Emancipation Proclamation

**3. Sherman's March to the Sea (1864)**
- Union General Sherman marched through Georgia
- Destroyed Confederate resources
- Helped end the war

**Important Documents:**

**Emancipation Proclamation (January 1, 1863)**
- Issued by President Lincoln
- Freed enslaved people in Confederate states
- Changed war goal to include ending slavery
- Allowed Black soldiers to join Union Army

**Gettysburg Address (November 1863)**
- Lincoln's famous short speech
- Honored soldiers who died at Gettysburg
- "Government of the people, by the people, for the people"

**How It Ended:**

**April 9, 1865:**
- Confederate General Robert E. Lee surrendered to Union General Ulysses S. Grant
- Surrender at Appomattox Court House, Virginia
- Other Confederate armies surrendered soon after

**April 14, 1865:**
- President Lincoln assassinated by John Wilkes Booth
- Just days after the war ended
- Nation mourned the loss

**After the War:**

**13th Amendment (1865):**
- Abolished slavery throughout United States
- Made slavery illegal everywhere

**14th Amendment (1868):**
- Gave citizenship to former slaves
- Equal protection under the law

**15th Amendment (1870):**
- Gave voting rights to Black men

**Reconstruction (1865-1877):**
- Period of rebuilding the South
- Integrating freed slaves into society
- Difficult and incomplete process
