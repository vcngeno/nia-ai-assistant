# The Founding Fathers

## The Men Who Created America

The Founding Fathers were the leaders who helped create the United States of America and wrote the Constitution!

**Key Facts:**
- "Founding Fathers" refers to leaders who founded the United States
- They wrote the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution
- They created a new type of government based on democracy
- Many were at the Constitutional Convention in Philadelphia (1787)
- They believed in liberty, democracy, and individual rights

**Major Founding Fathers:**

**George Washington (1732-1799)**
- "Father of His Country"
- Commander of Continental Army during Revolution
- First President of the United States (1789-1797)
- Set many presidential traditions
- Refused to become king
- On the $1 bill and quarter

**Thomas Jefferson (1743-1826)**
- Main author of Declaration of Independence
- Third President (1801-1809)
- Louisiana Purchase doubled size of US
- Founded University of Virginia
- On the $2 bill and nickel

**Benjamin Franklin (1706-1790)**
- Inventor (lightning rod, bifocals, Franklin stove)
- Diplomat who helped get French support
- Oldest signer of the Constitution (81 years old)
- Founded first public library in America
- On the $100 bill

**John Adams (1735-1826)**
- Helped write Declaration of Independence
- First Vice President under Washington
- Second President (1797-1801)
- Father of John Quincy Adams (6th President)
- Strong advocate for independence

**James Madison (1751-1836)**
- "Father of the Constitution"
- Wrote most of the Constitution
- Wrote the Bill of Rights (first 10 amendments)
- Fourth President (1809-1817)
- Shortest president (5 feet 4 inches tall!)

**Alexander Hamilton (1755-1804)**
- First Secretary of the Treasury
- Created national banking system
- Wrote many Federalist Papers
- Founded US Coast Guard
- On the $10 bill
- Subject of popular Broadway musical

**John Jay (1745-1829)**
- First Chief Justice of Supreme Court
- Helped write Federalist Papers
- Negotiated important treaties

**Samuel Adams (1722-1803)**
- Organized Boston Tea Party
- Strong voice for independence
- Cousin of John Adams

**Patrick Henry (1736-1799)**
- Famous speech: "Give me liberty, or give me death!"
- Virginia governor
- Powerful speaker for independence

**What They Created:**

**Declaration of Independence (1776)**
- Announced colonies' independence from Britain
- Written mainly by Jefferson
- Key idea: "All men are created equal"
- Lists reasons for independence

**US Constitution (1787)**
- Supreme law of the United States
- Creates three branches of government
- Defines how government works
- Can be amended (changed)
- Still used today!

**Bill of Rights (1791)**
- First 10 amendments to Constitution
- Protects individual freedoms
- Freedom of speech, religion, press
- Right to bear arms, fair trial, etc.

**Three Branches of Government:**
1. **Legislative** (Congress) - makes laws
2. **Executive** (President) - enforces laws
3. **Judicial** (Courts) - interprets laws

**Checks and Balances:**
- Each branch can limit the others' power
- Prevents any one branch from becoming too powerful
- Founding Fathers' solution to protect liberty

**Their Vision:**
- Democracy (people choose leaders)
- Republic (representatives make decisions)
- Individual rights protected by law
- No king or dictator
- Religious freedom
- Free speech and press
