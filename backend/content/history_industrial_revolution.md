# The Industrial Revolution in America

## How America Changed from Farms to Factories

The Industrial Revolution transformed America from a farming nation into an industrial powerhouse with factories and machines!

**Key Facts:**
- Occurred roughly from 1760s-1840s (First) and 1870s-1914 (Second)
- Changed how goods were made (by hand â†’ by machine)
- People moved from farms to cities for factory jobs
- Led to new inventions and technology
- Created both opportunities and challenges

**What Changed:**

**Before Industrial Revolution:**
- Most people lived on farms
- Made things by hand at home
- Small workshops and craftsmen
- Travel by horse or boat
- Life moved slowly

**After Industrial Revolution:**
- Many people worked in factories in cities
- Machines made things faster and cheaper
- Mass production of goods
- Railroads connected the country
- Life changed rapidly

**Important Inventions:**

**1. Cotton Gin (1793) - Eli Whitney**
- Cleaned cotton 50 times faster than by hand
- Made cotton very profitable
- Unfortunately increased demand for enslaved labor in South

**2. Steam Engine**
- Powered factories and trains
- Didn't need water power
- Factories could be built anywhere

**3. Telegraph (1837) - Samuel Morse**
- Sent messages instantly using electricity
- "What hath God wrought" - first message (1844)
- Connected the nation

**4. Sewing Machine (1846) - Elias Howe**
- Made clothing production much faster
- Textile industry boomed

**5. Telephone (1876) - Alexander Graham Bell**
- First words: "Mr. Watson, come here!"
- Revolutionized communication

**6. Light Bulb (1879) - Thomas Edison**
- Made electric lighting practical
- Extended working and living hours
- Edison Electric Company â†’ General Electric

**7. Automobile (1890s) - Henry Ford**
- Assembly line made cars affordable
- Model T: $850 â†’ $300
- Changed American life forever

**Factory System:**

**How It Worked:**
- Workers gathered in one building
- Machines did the work
- Division of labor (each person did one task)
- Mass production made goods cheaper

**Working Conditions:**
- Long hours (12-14 hours per day, 6 days a week)
- Low pay
- Dangerous machinery
- Child labor common
- No safety regulations at first

**Immigration:**
- Millions immigrated to America for factory jobs
- Ellis Island (New York) processed immigrants
- Many from Ireland, Italy, Eastern Europe, China
- Worked in factories, built railroads
- Brought diverse cultures to America

**Labor Movement:**
- Workers formed unions to demand better conditions
- Fought for 8-hour workday, weekends, safer conditions
- Famous strikes and protests
- Eventually won many rights

**Rise of Big Cities:**

**Urbanization:**
- People moved from farms to cities for jobs
- New York, Chicago, Philadelphia grew rapidly
- Tenement housing (crowded apartments)
- New infrastructure: sewers, streetlights, public transit

**Problems:**
- Overcrowding
- Pollution
- Disease spread
- Gap between rich and poor grew

**Captains of Industry/Robber Barons:**

**Andrew Carnegie - Steel**
- Built massive steel empire
- Later donated millions to libraries and schools

**John D. Rockefeller - Oil**
- Standard Oil Company
- Once richest man in America
- Later gave away much of his fortune

**J.P. Morgan - Banking**
- Financed railroads, companies
- Helped stabilize US economy

**Cornelius Vanderbilt - Railroads**
- Built railroad empire
- Connected America coast to coast

**Henry Ford - Automobiles**
- Assembly line production
- Made cars affordable for average Americans
- Paid workers well ($5/day)

**Positive Changes:**
- More goods available and cheaper
- New technologies improved life
- Economic growth and prosperity
- America became world industrial leader
- Middle class grew

**Negative Changes:**
- Pollution and environmental damage
- Dangerous working conditions
- Child labor
- Income inequality
- Poor living conditions in cities
