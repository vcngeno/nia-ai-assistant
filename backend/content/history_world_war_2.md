# World War II and America

## The War That Changed the World

World War II (1939-1945) was the largest and deadliest war in history, and America played a crucial role in winning it.

**Key Facts:**
- Lasted from 1939-1945 (6 years)
- Over 60 million people died worldwide
- Fought between Allies and Axis powers
- US entered after Pearl Harbor attack (December 7, 1941)
- Ended with atomic bombs on Japan (August 1945)

**The Two Sides:**

**Allies (Good Guys):**
- United States
- Great Britain
- Soviet Union (Russia)
- France
- China
- And many others

**Axis (Bad Guys):**
- Germany (led by Adolf Hitler)
- Japan
- Italy
- And their allies

**How It Started:**

**In Europe (1939):**
- Germany, led by dictator Adolf Hitler, invaded Poland
- Britain and France declared war on Germany
- Hitler wanted to conquer Europe
- Nazis believed in racial superiority (very wrong!)

**In Asia:**
- Japan wanted to control Asia and Pacific
- Invaded China and other countries
- Attacked European colonies

**America Enters the War:**

**Pearl Harbor - December 7, 1941**
- Japanese planes attacked US naval base in Hawaii
- Surprise attack on a Sunday morning
- Sank or damaged 8 battleships
- Over 2,400 Americans killed
- President Franklin D. Roosevelt: "A date which will live in infamy"
- America declared war the next day

**The War in Europe:**

**D-Day - June 6, 1944**
- Allied invasion of Nazi-occupied France
- Landed on beaches of Normandy
- Largest amphibious invasion in history
- Beginning of the end for Nazi Germany
- Led by General Dwight D. Eisenhower

**Battle of the Bulge (Winter 1944-45)**
- Germany's last major offensive
- Fought in harsh winter conditions
- US and Allies won

**Germany Surrenders - May 8, 1945 (V-E Day)**
- Hitler committed suicide
- Germany surrendered unconditionally
- Victory in Europe Day celebrated

**The War in Pacific:**

**Island Hopping:**
- US strategy to capture key Pacific islands
- Each island closer to Japan
- Famous battles: Midway, Iwo Jima, Okinawa
- Marines raised flag on Iwo Jima (famous photo)

**Atomic Bombs:**
- President Harry Truman faced difficult decision
- August 6, 1945: Hiroshima
- August 9, 1945: Nagasaki
- First and only use of nuclear weapons in war
- Japan surrendered days later

**Japan Surrenders - August 15, 1945 (V-J Day)**
- Victory over Japan Day
- War officially ended September 2, 1945
- Signed surrender on USS Missouri

**The Holocaust:**

**Nazi Genocide:**
- Hitler and Nazis tried to exterminate Jewish people
- 6 million Jews murdered
- Also killed Roma, disabled people, political opponents
- Concentration camps and death camps
- One of history's greatest atrocities

**Liberation:**
- Allied forces freed concentration camps
- World learned full horror of Holocaust
- "Never Again" became rallying cry
- Led to creation of Israel (1948)

**Home Front - America During the War:**

**Everyone Helped:**
- Factories switched to war production (tanks, planes, ships)
- Women worked in factories ("Rosie the Riveter")
- Rationing: limited sugar, meat, gasoline, rubber
- Victory gardens (growing own food)
- Buying war bonds to fund war effort
- Scrap metal drives

**Women's Roles:**
- Over 6 million women joined workforce
- Worked as welders, riveters, electricians
- Proved women could do "men's jobs"
- Changed women's role in society forever

**African Americans:**
- Tuskegee Airmen (Black fighter pilots)
- Fought in segregated units
- Proved their valor and skill
- Helped lead to later civil rights movement

**Japanese-American Internment:**
- 120,000 Japanese Americans forced into camps
- Lost homes, businesses, freedom
- Later recognized as great injustice
- US government apologized in 1988

**Important Leaders:**

**Franklin D. Roosevelt (FDR)**
- US President during most of war
- Led America through Great Depression and WWII
- Died April 1945, just before victory
- Only president elected four times

**Harry S. Truman**
- Became president after FDR died
- Made decision to use atomic bombs
- "The buck stops here"

**Winston Churchill**
- Prime Minister of Britain
- Inspired British resistance
- Famous speeches and leadership

**General Dwight D. Eisenhower**
- Led D-Day invasion
- Supreme Allied Commander in Europe
- Later became US President (1953-1961)

**General Douglas MacArthur**
- Led Pacific theater operations
- Accepted Japan's surrender
- "I shall return" (and he did!)

**Results of the War:**

**Immediate:**
- Axis powers defeated
- United Nations created to prevent future wars
- America and Soviet Union became superpowers
- Europe devastated and divided
- Cold War began

**Long-term:**
- America emerged as world leader
- Boom in American economy (1950s prosperity)
- Baby boom generation
- Civil rights movement gained momentum
- Nuclear age began
