# Measurement (US Customary Units)

## How We Measure Things in America

In the United States, we use customary units like inches, feet, pounds, and gallons to measure things!

**Key Facts:**
- The US uses the customary system (also called imperial units)
- Different tools measure different things
- Always include the unit in your answer (12 inches, not just 12)
- You can convert between different units
- Most other countries use the metric system, but the US primarily uses customary

**Length (How Long or Tall):**
- **Inch (in)** - about the length of your thumb
- **Foot (ft)** - 12 inches, about the length of your foot
- **Yard (yd)** - 3 feet or 36 inches
- **Mile (mi)** - 5,280 feet, used for long distances

**Conversions:**
- 1 foot = 12 inches
- 1 yard = 3 feet
- 1 mile = 1,760 yards

**Weight (How Heavy):**
- **Ounce (oz)** - a slice of bread weighs about 1 ounce
- **Pound (lb)** - 16 ounces, a loaf of bread weighs about 1 pound
- **Ton** - 2,000 pounds, about as heavy as a car

**Conversions:**
- 1 pound = 16 ounces
- 1 ton = 2,000 pounds

**Capacity (How Much Liquid):**
- **Cup (c)** - 8 fluid ounces
- **Pint (pt)** - 2 cups, a small milk carton
- **Quart (qt)** - 2 pints, a large milk carton
- **Gallon (gal)** - 4 quarts, a milk jug

**Conversions:**
- 1 pint = 2 cups
- 1 quart = 2 pints
- 1 gallon = 4 quarts
