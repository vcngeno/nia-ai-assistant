# The American Revolution

## How America Became Independent

The American Revolution was the war that made the 13 American colonies independent from Great Britain!

**Key Facts:**
- Fought from 1775-1783
- American colonies wanted freedom from British rule
- Led by General George Washington
- Resulted in the creation of the United States of America
- We celebrate Independence Day on July 4th

**Why Did It Start?**

**"No Taxation Without Representation!"**
- Britain taxed the colonists (Stamp Act, Tea Act)
- Colonists had no say in British government
- They wanted to make their own decisions
- Famous protest: Boston Tea Party (1773)

**Important Events:**

**1. Battles of Lexington and Concord (April 19, 1775)**
- First battles of the Revolution
- "The shot heard 'round the world"
- Paul Revere's midnight ride warning: "The British are coming!"

**2. Declaration of Independence (July 4, 1776)**
- Written mainly by Thomas Jefferson
- Declared America's independence from Britain
- Famous line: "All men are created equal"
- Signed by 56 Founding Fathers

**3. Valley Forge (Winter 1777-1778)**
- General Washington's army camped here
- Very cold, little food, many soldiers sick
- Soldiers stayed loyal despite hardships
- Army emerged stronger in spring

**4. Battle of Yorktown (1781)**
- Last major battle
- British General Cornwallis surrendered
- French navy helped Americans win
- Effectively ended the war

**5. Treaty of Paris (1783)**
- Officially ended the war
- Britain recognized American independence
- America gained land to the Mississippi River

**Key People:**

**American Patriots:**
- **George Washington** - Commander of Continental Army, 1st President
- **Thomas Jefferson** - Wrote Declaration of Independence
- **Benjamin Franklin** - Inventor, diplomat, helped get French support
- **John Adams** - Helped write Declaration, 2nd President
- **Patrick Henry** - "Give me liberty, or give me death!"
- **Paul Revere** - Famous midnight ride
- **Betsy Ross** - Said to have sewn first American flag

**British:**
- **King George III** - British king during Revolution
- **General Cornwallis** - British commander who surrendered

**The Result:**
- United States became an independent nation
- Constitution was written (1787)
- Bill of Rights added (1791)
- Set example for other independence movements

**Fun Fact:**
During the Battle of Trenton in 1776, George Washington and his troops crossed the icy Delaware River on Christmas night to surprise British soldiers!
