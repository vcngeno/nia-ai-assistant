# Westward Expansion

## Moving West: Building America from Coast to Coast

Westward Expansion was when Americans moved west across the continent to settle new lands in the 1800s.

**Key Facts:**
- Occurred mainly from 1803-1890s
- US grew from Atlantic coast to Pacific coast
- Driven by belief in "Manifest Destiny"
- Resulted in conflicts with Native Americans
- Changed America's geography, economy, and culture

**Major Events:**

**1. Louisiana Purchase (1803)**
- President Thomas Jefferson bought land from France
- Cost: $15 million (about 3 cents per acre!)
- Doubled the size of the United States
- Added land from Mississippi River to Rocky Mountains
- Included all or part of 15 current states

**2. Lewis and Clark Expedition (1804-1806)**
- Led by Meriwether Lewis and William Clark
- Explored Louisiana Purchase territory
- Reached Pacific Ocean
- Guided by Sacagawea, a Shoshone woman
- Mapped routes and documented plants/animals

**3. Oregon Trail (1840s-1860s)**
- 2,000-mile wagon route to Oregon
- About 400,000 pioneers traveled it
- Journey took 4-6 months
- Very difficult and dangerous
- Famous cry: "Oregon or Bust!"

**4. California Gold Rush (1849)**
- Gold discovered at Sutter's Mill, California (1848)
- 300,000 people rushed to California
- Called "forty-niners" (arrived in 1849)
- Few found gold, but California boomed
- California became a state in 1850

**5. Transcontinental Railroad (Completed 1869)**
- First railroad connecting East and West coasts
- Built by Central Pacific and Union Pacific
- Met at Promega Summit, Utah
- Journey from coast to coast: 6 months ‚Üí 6 days!
- Transformed trade and travel

**6. Homestead Act (1862)**
- Government gave 160 acres of land to settlers
- Had to farm it for 5 years to own it
- Over 1.6 million homesteads granted
- Encouraged western settlement

**Manifest Destiny:**
- Belief that US was destined to expand across continent
- "From sea to shining sea"
- Americans felt it was their right and duty
- Used to justify westward expansion

**Impact on Native Americans:**

**Tragedy and Conflict:**
- Native Americans had lived on these lands for thousands of years
- Lost their lands as settlers moved west
- Many forced onto reservations
- Buffalo (bison) nearly hunted to extinction
- Broken treaties and violent conflicts

**Trail of Tears (1838-1839):**
- Cherokee and other tribes forced to relocate
- Marched from Southeast to Oklahoma
- Thousands died from cold, hunger, disease
- One of darkest chapters in US history

**Life on the Frontier:**

**Pioneers:**
- Traveled in covered wagons
- Faced harsh weather, disease, accidents
- Built log cabins and sod houses
- Grew their own food
- Self-reliant and resourceful

**Cowboys:**
- Herded cattle across open range
- Cattle drives from Texas to railroads
- Hard, dangerous work
- Both Black and White cowboys
- Became American icons

**Frontier Towns:**
- Mining towns sprang up quickly
- Often lawless ("Wild West")
- Famous lawmen: Wyatt Earp, Wild Bill Hickok
- Eventually became civilized communities

**New States:**
- California (1850)
- Oregon (1859)
- Nevada (1864)
- Colorado (1876)
- And many more!

**The Frontier Closes (1890s):**
- Census Bureau declared frontier "closed"
- Continent settled from coast to coast
- End of an era in American history
