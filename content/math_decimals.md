# Decimals

## What Are Decimals?

Decimals are another way to show parts of a whole, using place value! They're closely related to fractions.

**Key Facts:**
- Decimals use a dot (.) called a "decimal point"
- Numbers to the right of the decimal point are parts less than one
- Each place to the right is ten times smaller

- Money uses decimals: $3.50 means 3 dollars and 50 cents

**Real-World Examples:**
- **Money**: $5.75 (5 dollars and 75 cents)
- **Measurements**: 1.5 meters, 3.2 liters
- **Sports**: 9.8 seconds in a race
- **Grades**: 89.5% on a test

**Reading Decimals:**
- 0.5 = "five tenths" or "zero point five"
- 0.25 = "twenty-five hundredths" or "zero point two five"
- 3.14 = "three and fourteen hundredths" or "three point one four"

**Comparing Decimals:**
1. Compare whole numbers first (3.5 vs 2.9: 3 is bigger)
2. Then compare tenths place (0.6 vs 0.4: 0.6 is bigger)
3. Then hundredths, and so on

**Adding/Subtracting Decimals:**
- Line up the decimal points!
- Add zeros if needed to make same length
```
  3.50
+ 2.75
------
  6.25
```
