# Multiplication

## What is Multiplication?

Multiplication is a fast way to add the same number many times! Instead of adding 5+5+5, we can say 3√ó5=15.

**Key Facts:**
- Multiplication is repeated addition
- The √ó symbol means "times" or "groups of"
- The numbers we multiply are called "factors"
- The answer is called the "product"
- Any number times 1 equals itself (7√ó1=7)
- Any number times 0 equals 0 (7√ó0=0)

**Understanding as Groups:**
- 4√ó3 means "4 groups of 3" or "3 added 4 times"
- Picture 4 baskets with 3 apples each = 12 apples total
- 5√ó2 means 5 pairs (like 5 pairs of shoes = 10 shoes)

**Real-World Examples:**
- Each pizza has 8 slices, you order 3 pizzas: 3√ó8=24 slices
- You save $5 per week for 6 weeks: 6√ó5=$30 saved
- A carton holds 12 eggs, you buy 4 cartons: 4√ó12=48 eggs
- Soccer practice is 2 hours per day, 5 days a week: 5√ó2=10 hours

**Multiplication Tricks:**
- **2s**: Double the number (2√ó6 = 6+6 = 12)
- **5s**: Count by fives or think "half of 10s"
- **9s**: The digits in the answer add up to 9 (9√ó3=27, 2+7=9)
- **10s**: Just add a zero! (10√ó4=40)
- **Commutative**: 3√ó4 = 4√ó3 (order doesn't matter!)
