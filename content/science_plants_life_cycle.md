# Plant Life Cycles

## How Plants Grow and Reproduce

Plants go through different stages of life, just like animals do! Understanding plant life cycles helps us grow food and appreciate nature.

**Key Facts:**
- All plants start as seeds (except ferns and mosses)
- Plants need water, sunlight, air, and nutrients to grow
- Plants make their own food through photosynthesis
- Different plants have different life cycles
- Some plants live for one year, others live for hundreds of years

**Parts of a Plant:**

**Roots:**
- Grow underground
- Absorb water and nutrients from soil
- Anchor plant in place
- Some roots store food (carrots, beets)

**Stem:**
- Supports the plant
- Carries water and nutrients up to leaves
- Carries food from leaves to rest of plant
- Can be soft (flowers) or hard (trees)

**Leaves:**
- Make food through photosynthesis
- Contain chlorophyll (makes them green)
- Have tiny holes (stomata) for breathing
- Come in many shapes and sizes

**Flowers:**
- Reproductive part of plant
- Attract pollinators (bees, butterflies, birds)
- Contain male parts (stamens) and female parts (pistils)
- Beautiful colors and smells

**Seeds/Fruits:**
- Develop from flowers after pollination
- Seeds contain baby plant (embryo) and food
- Fruits protect seeds and help spread them

**Basic Plant Life Cycle:**

**1. Seed Stage:**
- Seeds contain baby plant and stored food
- Have protective outer coat
- Can survive long periods waiting for right conditions
- Some seeds very tiny, others large (coconut!)

**2. Germination (Sprouting):**
- Seed begins to grow when conditions are right
- Needs water, warmth, and sometimes light
- Seed coat breaks open
- Root grows down first
- Shoot grows up toward light

**3. Seedling Stage:**
- Young plant emerges from soil
- First leaves appear
- Starts making own food through photosynthesis
- Grows roots, stem, and leaves

**4. Adult/Mature Plant:**
- Fully grown
- Has many leaves to make food
- Strong roots and stem
- Ready to reproduce

**5. Flowering Stage:**
- Plant produces flowers (flowering plants)
- Flowers contain reproductive parts
- Attract pollinators
- Some plants flower once, others many times

**6. Pollination:**
- Pollen moves from one flower to another
- Can be done by insects, birds, wind, water
- Necessary for making seeds
- Bees are important pollinators!

**7. Seed Formation:**
- After pollination, seeds form
- Fruits develop around seeds
- Seeds mature and are ready to spread

**8. Seed Dispersal:**
- Seeds spread away from parent plant
- Methods: wind (dandelions, maples), water (coconuts), animals (berries, burrs), explosion (popcorn!)

**Types of Life Cycles:**

**Annuals (1 year):**
- Complete life cycle in one growing season
- Die after producing seeds
- Examples: marigolds, tomatoes, corn, sunflowers
- Must be replanted each year

**Biennials (2 years):**
- First year: grow leaves and roots
- Second year: flower, make seeds, then die
- Examples: carrots, parsley, cabbage

**Perennials (many years):**
- Live for many years
- Die back in winter, regrow in spring
- Examples: trees, roses, tulips, strawberries
- Don't need replanting

**For Humans:**

- Produce oxygen we breathe
- Provide materials (wood, cotton, paper)
- Make medicines
- Create beauty in nature

**Plant Adaptations:**

**Desert Plants (Cacti):**
- Store water in thick stems
- Have spines instead of leaves (less water loss)
- Deep or widespread roots
- Waxy coating prevents water loss

**Rainforest Plants:**
- Large leaves to catch sunlight
- Drip tips to shed heavy rain
- Vines climb to reach light
- Some grow on other plants (epiphytes)

**Wetland Plants:**
- Roots adapted to wet soil
- Can tolerate being underwater
- Examples: cattails, water lilies

**Arctic/Alpine Plants:**
- Low-growing to avoid wind
- Short growing season
- Hairy leaves for warmth
- Bright flowers to attract scarce pollinators

2. **Water** - Keep soil moist but not soggy
3. **Air** - Good air circulation
4. **Nutrients** - From soil or fertilizer
