# Animals and Habitats of the United States

## Amazing Wildlife Across America

The United States is home to incredible animals living in many different habitats!

**Key Facts:**
- A habitat is where an animal naturally lives
- The US has deserts, forests, mountains, oceans, plains, and more
- Animals are adapted to survive in their specific habitats
- Many US animals are protected by conservation laws
- The bald eagle is America's national bird

**Major US Habitats and Animals:**

**1. Eastern Forests (Appalachian Mountains, New England)**
- **White-tailed deer** - most common large mammal
- **Black bear** - can climb trees, hibernate in winter
- **Red fox** - clever hunters
- **Wild turkey** - Ben Franklin wanted this as national bird!
- **Raccoon** - masked nighttime feeders

**2. Western Mountains (Rocky Mountains, Sierra Nevada)**
- **Grizzly bear** - powerful and large
- **Mountain lion (cougar)** - solitary hunters
- **Bighorn sheep** - amazing climbers
- **Elk** - large deer with big antlers
- **Golden eagle** - incredible eyesight

**3. Great Plains (Midwest)**
- **American bison (buffalo)** - once nearly extinct, now recovering
- **Prairie dog** - live in underground "towns"
- **Pronghorn** - second-fastest land animal (after cheetah)
- **Coyote** - adaptable and intelligent
- **Burrowing owl** - lives underground

**4. Southwestern Deserts (Arizona, New Mexico)**
- **Roadrunner** - can run 20 mph
- **Gila monster** - venomous lizard
- **Javelina** - looks like a pig
- **Rattlesnake** - venomous, has warning rattle
- **Desert tortoise** - can live 80+ years

**5. Pacific Coast (California, Oregon, Washington)**
- **Sea otter** - uses tools to open shells
- **Gray whale** - migrates 12,000 miles each year
- **Sea lion** - playful and loud
- **Salmon** - swim upstream to spawn
- **Bald eagle** - national bird, amazing fisher

**6. Southeast Wetlands (Everglades, Louisiana bayous)**
- **American alligator** - can live 50+ years
- **Manatee** - gentle "sea cow"
- **Roseate spoonbill** - pink wading bird
- **Snapping turtle** - powerful jaws
- **Wood stork** - only stork native to US

**7. Alaska**
- **Polar bear** - excellent swimmers
- **Moose** - largest deer species
- **Arctic fox** - white fur in winter
- **Humpback whale** - famous for singing
- **Caribou** - migrate in huge herds

**Endangered Species Success Stories:**
- **Bald eagle** - removed from endangered list in 2007
- **Gray wolf** - successfully reintroduced to Yellowstone
- **California condor** - saved from extinction through breeding programs

**National Parks and Wildlife:**
- Yellowstone (first national park, 1872)
- Everglades
- Yosemite
- Grand Canyon
- Great Smoky Mountains
