# Weather and Climate in America

## Understanding US Weather Patterns

The United States has incredibly diverse weather because it's such a big country!

**Key Facts:**
- Weather is what's happening right now (rainy, sunny, snowy)
- Climate is the typical weather pattern over many years
- The US has almost every type of climate in the world
- Weather is measured in Fahrenheit (Â°F) in America
- The National Weather Service tracks and predicts weather

**US Weather Phenomena:**

**Tornadoes:**
- Most common in "Tornado Alley" (Great Plains states)
- Kansas, Oklahoma, Texas, Nebraska
- Spring and early summer

**Hurricanes:**
- Hit the Atlantic coast and Gulf of Mexico
- Florida, Louisiana, Texas, Carolinas
- Hurricane season: June-November
- Measured by categories 1-5

**Blizzards:**
- Heavy snow and strong winds
- Common in Northeast and Midwest
- Can close schools and roads

**Thunderstorms:**
- Happen everywhere in the US
- Most common in summer
- Lightning, thunder, heavy rain

- **Weather**: "It's snowing today!" (short-term)
- **Climate**: "Minnesota has cold winters" (long-term pattern)
